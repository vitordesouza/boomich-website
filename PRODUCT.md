# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Clients buying outcomes: founders, product and engineering leads, and agencies deciding whether to trust Boomich with a complex project. They usually arrive on a phone, from a referral or a proposal, and they are qualifying one thing: is this studio serious enough for hard work?

Secondary audience: engineers and recruiters inspecting the work itself, including this repository. The codebase is deliberately part of the pitch.

## Product Purpose

The marketing site and brand system for Boomich, an independent software studio. It is the professional vehicle of one senior engineer (João Vitor de Souza) operating through a Portuguese LDA. Boomich takes on hard technical work: realtime systems, cross-platform mobile, applied AI. Projects where the difficulty is the deliverable.

Success: a qualified visitor reads the page, believes this is senior engineering that can carry a complex project, and initiates contact. A recruiter or engineer screenshots the site and sends it to a colleague asking "how was this made?"

## Brand Concept

**LOAD-BEARING.** A boom is the member of a crane that carries the load. Boomich is the load-bearing member of a product effort: the part between intent and reality that holds when weight is applied. The brand does not say this in copy. It demonstrates it.

Visual world: metrology and structural-test equipment. Load cells, strain gauges, calibration plates, test fixtures. Precision instruments, not construction machinery. Never hazard stripes, stencil type, rivets, blueprints, cranes, or industrial decor. The reference is the quiet lab where things are measured, not the loud site where things are welded.

The one image is a working instrument: an interactive truss the visitor can load, with a live readout. The site behaves like a calibrated device: markings correspond to real values, state is exposed, limits are defined.

## Brand Personality

**Calibrated · Load-rated · Quietly confident.**

Voice: a senior engineer who has seen production fires and prevents them. Studio voice ("we", "Boomich"), never fabricating a team. Short sentences. Almost no adjectives; specificity carries the weight. Copy talks about constraints, latency, failure modes, and recovery, not about passion. No emoji. Sentence case headlines. Uppercase only inside instrument readouts, where it is a measurement label, not decoration.

Emotional goal: the calm of handing a heavy thing to someone who visibly holds it.

## Anti-references

- **The 2024-26 AI-default dev-portfolio formula**: dark hero + heavy grotesque + red accent + mono uppercase eyebrows on every section + numbered section scaffolding (01/02/03) + warm-paper background. This was the old brand. It is banned.
- **Editorial-typographic lane**: display serif, italics, mono metadata, hairline monochrome restraint. Equally saturated. Banned.
- **Terminal-native dark mode**: green-on-black, scanlines, cursor blinks. Banned.
- **Construction LARP**: hazard chevrons, stencil fonts, blueprint grids, crane silhouettes.
- **SaaS clichés**: gradient heroes, glassmorphism, stat blocks, identical card grids, testimonial carousels.
- **Fabricated proof**: invented clients, testimonials, metrics, team members, or fake report numbers. Real facts only, anonymized under NDA where required.
- **Stock photography**: none, ever. The instrument is the imagery.

## Design Principles

1. **The instrument is the argument.** The hero artifact and every interactive detail must actually work, measure something real on screen, and hold up under inspection of the source. If a detail fakes precision, cut it.
2. **Calibrated restraint.** One environment (deep mineral olive), one signal color (calibration amber) reserved exclusively for live values, measurements, and state changes. Amber never decorates.
3. **Machined, not drawn.** Edges are flush, joins are mitered, corners are chamfered like edge breaks on milled parts. Depth comes from tone steps and engraved hairlines, never shadows or gradients.
4. **Specifics over adjectives.** Copy names the hard thing. Design shows the discipline.
5. **Excellent on a phone first.** The qualifying visitor reads on a phone. The instrument must run at 60fps there, degrade gracefully, and respect reduced motion.
6. **The repo is the pitch.** Structure, tests, CI, documented tradeoffs. Engineers will read this code; it is a brand surface.
